Core concepts

  • Wave–particle duality: light and matter show both particle and wave behavior.
  • Superposition: a system can exist in multiple states at once until measured.
  • Uncertainty: certain pairs of properties (like position and momentum) cannot both be known precisely.
  • Entanglement: particles share linked states regardless of distance.
  • Measurement: observation changes what is known about the system.
